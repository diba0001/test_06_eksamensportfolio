04 FUNDAMENTAL ANIMATION / Go to site

In Theme 04, Basic Animation, we learned about various design principles and animational designs such as flat design, kawaii, baby bias, bouba and Kiki, asymmetry, etc. I chose to use the bouba principle to solve the subsequent task by designing my game, "How to Feta", with mainly round and child-friendly shapes, opposite to the sharp and angular shapes of Kiki.

I opted for this principle in my solution instead of kawaii or Kiki because I wanted more cartoon-like characters and landscapes. Kawaii refers to the cute, and Kiki to the sharp, and I felt that neither of these directions aligned with the design I envisioned for my game.

Additionally, we learned about planning and structuring the design process, keeping the user and the task's goals in mind. I applied this knowledge to transition from idea to product, but looking back at the task, I approached it differently than I would have today.

I started by drawing my characters before I had sketched my game pages or brainstormed my ideas. This led me to realize just before the deadline that I had i.e. completely overlooked the rule of thirds. My game pages did not have three layers and, therefore, did not feel super abstract or well-thought-out.

This is the key lesson I took from that theme – it's important to start from scratch, brainstorm, and investigate the task before diving into drawing and coding. This made my project start at an unusual point in the process, and as a result, I felt like I was constantly struggling to maintain a coherent thread through the design, coding, and potential development of the game.

The development process for the task in this theme can be seen in the attached screenshots below. Personally, I found it most challenging to grasp JavaScript, so I was particularly focused on improving my skills in the subtasks assigned, and especially in the redesign I carried out on my portfolio site from Theme 03 (as mentioned earlier).

JavaScript was an entirely new world of possibilities, and it was an overwhelming start to access that part of the virtual sandbox in a project like this, where my focus also lies in maintaining design principles and conventions, gestalt laws, and layout principles from previous projects and themes.
