05.1 FUNDEMENTAL VIDEO / Go to site

In addition to theme 05, fundamental content, we also got introduced to basic video-production & Adobe Premiere Pro. The idea was for us to learn simple video-production tools and usage of these. We got introduced to the basics of editing, grading and shooting. More specifically to interview-guides, storyboarding, Adobe PP color-grading, the 5-shots-rule, rule-of-thirds, overlays etc.

I especially used tools like; Film-dissolve, adjustment-layers, Exponential audio gain, Constant Gain, Dip to Black, Dip to White, clip-speed, color-enhancer, Crossfade, lumetri color presets, cold-color-correction & -grade in my productions and postproductions.

I decided to use the film-dissolve and dip-to´s to create smoother transitions in my interview, the reason especially being that the clips were very stationary and boring, hence it being an interview on a couch.

I used the audio techniques of exponential gain, constant gain and crossfade to unite and align the sound to one single level, as the mic picked up various different levels depending on how the interview-person sat and spoke. I tried to grade as best as I could with some cold and minor warm color-grading & correction, as the room was quite dark and monochrome.

I will get back to the errors of this in my reflections below. Clip speed and rule-of-thirds + b-roll were used to create transitioning shots as the interviewed changed form and/or subject. Also, having a b-roll can help the viewer stay longer / become more interested in the film, as it changes up the setting & scene, while still having the same sound of the a-roll.

Looking back, I had quite an obstacle with the first video in its post-production. I write, shoot and direct short-films in my free-time, and hence this, I was very angry at the overexposure of the video (Retfærdighedens rytter, theme 05.1).

The overexposure happened because we shot it with HDR on an iPhone, which is incompatible with the H.264 file format & settings of Adobe PP - and therefore will make the video turn yellowish/incredibly overexposed once portrayed.

I tried to export in Apple formats (QuickTime in Apple ProRes HQ HLG) back to the Adobe PP standard format of (H. 264) - which is 4K. Hence this, I have exported the video in a monochrome to show how this also will stain the project with an overexposure of light, even in a monochrome version. I truly regret using an iPhone to shoot a video with, as Adobe & Mac rarely work well together.

I am used to work with .raw-files from BlackMagics in my freetime. The smart thing about raw-files is that they’re easily compatible with most programs and primarily used to re-export into other formats of choice.
