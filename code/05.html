05 FUNDAMENTAL CONTENT / Go to site

In Theme 05, Basic Content, the main task was to redesign a website for a self-selected company. We chose to redesign an internal documentation site for Europe's largest hearing aid company, WIDEX.

In this theme, we had to independently apply the processes, tools, and techniques we had learned through the previous themes. For an example, this meant using tools from theme 03, UX/UI, to research and collect data. We employed BERT tests and surveys to gather internal data within the company. Subsequently, we used 5-second tests and observation tests to evaluate our redesign with new users outside of the company.

This research quickly revealed that our primary challenges in the redesign lay in two aspects: establishing a consistent visual language between the company's external and internal sites and making the internal site easier to navigate and more user-friendly.

To address these two main tasks, we specifically drew on our knowledge from theme 02 about design principles and gestalt laws, etc., to design the site's layout, structure, and local & global elements. By this, we mean that we set up grids to structure the site's content and redesigned all global elements such as navigation, burger menu, and links to make them clearer and straightforward.

Additionally, we implemented local elements like mouse trails and buttons to provide users with a better overview of their virtual location and footsteps. I used my knowledge of minimalist design from previous themes to make the header & menu, and my new knowledge of iFrames to attach .PDF’s to the site.

Since this was our first complete group project of the semester, I have quite a few reflections to note. The project offered invaluable insights into the dynamics of collaborative work, shedding light on the diverse ways in which individuals approach tasks and projects. One major takeaway was the realization that these varied working styles did lead to miscommunication, resulting in a final product that lacked cohesion and alignment in some areas.

Navigating through a group project underscored the importance of effective communication and understanding each team member's strengths and working preferences. Where some would use percentages in margins, others would use rem, where some would use one header, others would user another etc.

It felt as if we just let eachother do what we wanted and felt good at, and then met a few days before the deadline to gather our work. Looking back, this is not what was intended with this assignment & theme, as we regularly got taught in using scrums, dailies & a group-contract to avoid any problems and internal divide.

In essence, this first group project served as a learning ground for understanding the complexities of collaboration, emphasizing the need for effective communication, adaptability, and a shared commitment to the project's goals.

I feel like we created what we wanted, and aligned the internal site to the external one, but that our teamwork was a bit too squared at times. These insights will undoubtedly shape my approach to future group endeavors - both in the professional, personal and educational realms.
